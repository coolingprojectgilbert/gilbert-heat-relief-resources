Need a Private Contractor? (Non-Subsidized)

If you do not qualify for the assistance programs above and need to hire a private company for AC repair or replacement, it is crucial to follow these steps:

  • Verify License: Always check that the contractor is licensed by the Arizona Registrar of Contractors (ROC) before signing any contract.
  • Get Multiple Quotes: Obtain at least three written bids before committing to any major work.
  • Check Reputation: Look up reviews and ratings on the Better Business Bureau (BBB) or other local sites.

Prevention & Heat Safety Tips

Know the signs of heat illness and take immediate action.

Heat Exhaustion vs. Heat Stroke

  • Exhaustion: Heavy sweating, paleness, muscle cramps, vomiting. Action: Move to a cool place, sip water.
  • Stroke (EMERGENCY): High body temperature (103¬∞F+), red/hot/dry skin (no sweat), confusion, unconsciousness. Action: Call 911 immediately.

Hydration and Cooling

  • Drink water before you feel thirsty; avoid sugary or alcoholic drinks.
  • Wear light-colored, loose-fitting clothing.
  • Take cool showers or baths to lower body temperature rapidly.
  • Use a misting bottle or wet towel on your neck/wrists.

Energy Saving Tips

  • Set your thermostat as high as comfortably possible (e.g., 78¬∞F).
  • Use ceiling fans to supplement AC, but turn them off when leaving a room.
  • Close blinds and curtains during the day to block sun exposure.
